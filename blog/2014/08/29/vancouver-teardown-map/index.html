Vancouver Teardown Map

There has been lots of talk about old homes being torn down and replaced in Vancouver. In the past decades land prices have risen sharply and the building stock has not had time to catch up. A significant portion of Vancouver consists of land that is by an order of magnitude more valuable than the building on top of it.

For example, a buyer spending $1.3M for a single family house worth $50k will have to tear down and rebuild, renovate extensively or make some small upgrades. From as investment perspective, the economically most sound solution usually is to tear down and rebuild. Money invested in small upgrades will probably see zero returns if resold, and extensive renovations face many hurdles to convert an old house with small rooms into more of an open living environment that the market values. And the “investment perspective” usually takes over in decision making since in most cases their property is by far their largest investment.

So how many “teardowns” are there in Vancouver where the house is less than, say, 5% of the total value? And where in the city are these houses? We turned to Vancouver’s open data to build an interactive map to explore the low end of the building stock.

The short answer is that almost one in three properties in Vancouver fall into this category. And they are distributed quite evenly across all of Vancouver with some areas like downtown looking a little better.
